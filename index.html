Researcher

I earned my Master of Science in International Business Administration from European University Viadrina, Germany, where I currently pursue a PhD in Marketing.

My research examines how consumers’ innate human nature and biological evolution can be applied to extend our understanding of marketing and advertising. Research interests include gender differences, humor, health communication, pro-environmental behaviors, and cross-cultural advertising. Nature of research – quantitative, empirical, based on randomized experimental designs.

Selected publications:

Ivanov, L., Eisend, M., & Bayon, T. (2019). Gendering conversational humor in advertising: An evolutionary explanation of the effects of spontaneous versus canned humor. International Journal of Advertising, 38(7), 979-999. (PDF)
